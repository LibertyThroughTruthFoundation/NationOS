1.  **The Nature of the Texts:** The Apocalypse of Peter is a **visionary, prophetic** text—it is meant to shock the conscience and reveal the cosmic stakes of judgment and redemption. The Book of Clement (likely the *First Epistle of Clement to the Corinthians*) is a **pastoral, ecclesiastical** text. It deals with church order, apostolic succession, and resolving conflict within the body. Its tone is necessarily different because its purpose is different: it's about building the practical, day-to-day life of the covenant community. This is the "how" after the Apocalypse's "what."

2.  **The Correct Next Step: The Mashafa Kidan (Book of the Covenant):** Your instinct is precisely right. The Book of the Covenant is the logical and crucial next step. If the Apocalypse of Peter shows you the **dangers of hypocrisy and the reality of judgment**, and Clement shows you the **need for godly order**, the Mashafa Kidan is traditionally held to contain the **post-resurrection teachings of Jesus** that equip the church to navigate both.

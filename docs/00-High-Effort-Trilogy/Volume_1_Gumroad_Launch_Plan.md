## 2. PRICING STRATEGY

Based on research into nonfiction theology ebook pricing, here is the recommended strategy:

### **Recommended Price: $9.99**

**Why $9.99?**
- **Nonfiction Sweet Spot:** Research shows that full-length nonfiction ebooks typically price between $5.99 and $9.99 [1]
- **Value Signal:** $9.99 signals that this is a substantial, high-value work (not a quick guide)
- **Comparable Theology Books:** Professional theology ebooks from publishers like Eerdmans and Banner of Truth range from $8-$30 [2] [3]
- **70% Royalty:** On Gumroad, you keep 90% of the sale price (minus payment processing fees), so $9.99 nets you approximately $8.90 per sale

### **Alternative Pricing Options:**

| Price Point | Strategy | When to Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **$7.99** | Lower barrier to entry | If you want to maximize volume for a new author launch |
| **$9.99** | Standard nonfiction pricing | Recommended for MVP launch (signals quality) |
| **$12.99** | Premium pricing | After Volume 1 has proven sales and reviews |
| **Pay What You Want (min $7.99)** | Experimental | To test price sensitivity and build goodwill |

**Recommendation:** Start with **$9.99 fixed price** for the MVP launch. After gathering feedback and refining to the "100% version," consider raising to $12.99 for the second edition.

## 4. EMAIL CAPTURE STRATEGY

Building an email list is critical for launching Volume 2 and creating a sustainable author platform.

### **Strategy 1: Gumroad Email Collection (Built-In)**
- Gumroad automatically captures the email of every customer
- After purchase, customers can opt-in to receive updates from you
- Use this list to announce Volume 2, offer discounts, and share exclusive content

### **Strategy 2: Lead Magnet (Free Sample)**
- Offer the first 2-3 chapters as a **free PDF download** in exchange for an email address
- Use a tool like **ConvertKit** or **Mailchimp** (free tiers available) to manage your list
- Link to this free sample from your social media, blog posts, and community forums

### **Strategy 3: Post-Purchase Email Sequence**
After someone buys Volume 1, send them a 3-email sequence:

1. **Email 1 (Immediate):** Thank you + download link + invitation to join the email list for Volume 2 updates
2. **Email 2 (3 days later):** "What did you think of Chapter 4?" + request for feedback/review
3. **Email 3 (7 days later):** "The story behind Volume 2" + exclusive sneak peek + pre-order offer

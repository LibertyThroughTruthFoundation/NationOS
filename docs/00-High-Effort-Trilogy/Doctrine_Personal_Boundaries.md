## 1. THE CORE PRINCIPLE: ENMESHED & HONORABLE

The central insight is that **doctrine and personal testimony are inseparable**. The theology is not abstract; it is proven and refined through the lived experience of the author. The journey of falling, failing, and being met with grace is the tangible proof of the "Merciful Middle."

However, this transparency must be governed by honor. We will be transparent about the author's journey while honoring the boundaries of family, friends, and the community.

*   **Enmeshed:** The personal journey will be woven into the narrative as pastoral testimony, illustrating the doctrine in action.
*   **Honorable:** This testimony will be anonymized where necessary, protecting the privacy of individuals and avoiding prescriptive "do as I did" formulas.

## 3. GUIDELINES FOR WRITING (VOLUME 1 & BEYOND)

1.  **Anonymize All Individuals:** No real names of family, friends, or church members will be used. Use archetypal descriptions (e.g., "a potential bride," "a brother in the faith," "my former spouse").
2.  **Focus on the Principle, Not the Person:** When sharing a personal story, the goal is to illustrate a theological principle (e.g., grace, jurisdictional boundaries), not to detail a personal grievance or drama.
3.  **Avoid Prescriptive Language:** Never say "you should do what I did." Instead, use pastoral language: "In my journey, I found peace by seeking the Lord in this way... I encourage you to seek Him for your own path."
4.  **Separate Logistics from Doctrine:** Details about housing, finances, and daily logistics are for private records and communication. They will not be included in the published theological works.

By adhering to this framework, the High-Effort Trilogy will maintain its theological integrity and prophetic power while demonstrating the highest level of honor and pastoral care.

## PART 3: JURISDICTIONAL ANALYSIS

### De Facto vs. De Jure Framework

**De Jure (By Right):**
- **Righteous Law:** Reflects God's moral order (Proverbs 10:2 - "Ill-gotten treasures have no lasting value")
- **Example:** Murder prohibition (Genesis 9:6 - "Whoever sheds human blood, by humans shall their blood be shed")

**De Facto (By Fact):**
- **Established Authority:** Exists by power, not necessarily righteousness
- **Example:** Unjust taxation (1 Samuel 8:10-18 - King will take your sons, daughters, fields)

**Mexico Vape Ban Assessment:**
- **De Facto:** Established by governing authorities (General Health Law 2026)
- **De Jure Righteousness?** Debatable - vape ban may be paternalistic overreach (harm reduction vs. prohibition)
- **Romans 13 Application:** Submit to **de facto authority** unless it **contradicts God's command** (Acts 5:29)

**Conclusion:** Mexico vape ban is **de facto authority** (established law), and Romans 13:1-7 requires submission **even if not de jure righteous**, because cannabis use is **permissible** (not commanded).

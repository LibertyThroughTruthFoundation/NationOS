### The Architecture of the Leasehold

To understand how the Squatter Regime stole your property, you must understand the legal architecture of the leasehold.

In the original American republic, land was held in **allodial title**. Allodial title means absolute, unconditional ownership. The land belongs to you. No government, no bank, no institution can place a lien on it, tax it, or seize it. It is your sovereign domain.

The De Facto system systematically replaced allodial title with **fee simple title**. Fee simple sounds impressive—it sounds like ownership. But "fee" is the operative word. Fee simple title means you hold the land subject to the conditions imposed by the sovereign (in this case, the state). Those conditions include property taxes, zoning regulations, building codes, and eminent domain.

Under fee simple title, the state is the ultimate owner of the land. You are merely the tenant. You hold the land at the pleasure of the sovereign, and the sovereign can revoke your tenancy at any time for failure to pay the tribute or for violation of the conditions.

This is the legal reality that most Americans have never been told. The deed in your filing cabinet is not a deed of ownership; it is a deed of tenancy.

### The Mortgage: A Death Pledge

The word "mortgage" comes from the Old French *mort gage*, which literally translates to "death pledge." 

This is not a coincidence.

When you sign a mortgage, you are pledging your future labor—your life energy, your time on this earth—as collateral for a debt. You are promising to pull the oar for thirty years in exchange for the privilege of occupying a property that the bank technically owns until you have fulfilled the pledge.

During those thirty years, the bank holds the title. If you miss payments, the bank takes the property. If you make every payment on time, you will have paid the bank approximately two to three times the original purchase price by the time the mortgage is satisfied.

And even after you have paid off the mortgage and the bank releases the title, you still do not own the property in the allodial sense. You still owe the annual tribute to the county assessor. You are still a tenant.

The death pledge is a masterpiece of extraction. It captures your labor for three decades, generates massive interest income for the banking cartel, and at the end of it all, you still do not own what you paid for.

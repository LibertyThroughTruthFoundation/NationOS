## 1. CANONICAL FOUNDATION

### 1.1. Ethiopian Orthodox Canon (LXX + DSS)

**Core Thesis:**
The Ethiopian Orthodox Church preserved the fuller canonical tradition that includes books removed or marginalized by the Masoretic Text tradition.

**Key Texts:**
- **1 Enoch** - Angelology, cosmology, Watchers narrative
- **Jubilees** - Calendar, covenant law, chronology
- **Fuller Daniel** - Additional chapters and context
- **Septuagint (LXX)** - Greek translation reflecting pre-Masoretic Hebrew text tradition
- **Dead Sea Scrolls (DSS)** - Archaeological validation of LXX canon

**Implications:**
- The supernatural reality (angels, principalities, sons of God) is central, not fringe
- Covenant-keeping nationalism is biblical (Deuteronomy 23:3-8, ethnos/binary distinctions)
- The "gates" framework (who enters the assembly) is textually grounded

## PART 1: THE LIQUIDATION PHASE (AD 30–70)

### Administrative Status: The "Notice to Quit"

The Liquidation Phase is the 40-year period between Christ's crucifixion and the destruction of the Temple. During this era, the legal verdict was issued, but the physical infrastructure of the Old Covenant administration remained standing.

At the Cross (AD 30), Christ served an eviction notice to the principalities and powers (*archai/exousias*). The debt that had enslaved humanity was nailed to the Cross (Colossians 2:14). The principalities and powers were "disarmed" and "put to open shame" (Colossians 2:15).

But the Temple still stood. The Levitical priesthood still operated. The Mosaic Law still governed Israel. The old management had received notice, but they had not yet vacated the premises.

### The Conflict: The Advance Team

The first-century Church operated as an "Advance Team," tasked with declaring the new administration while the old infrastructure was still physically present. This created intense conflict:

- **The Apostles vs. the Sanhedrin:** The Jewish leadership, understanding that the new administration threatened their power, persecuted the Church.
- **The Church vs. Rome:** The Roman Empire, representing the consolidated power of the fallen principalities, persecuted the Church.
- **The Tension:** Believers lived in a liminal space. The legal victory had been won, but the old order had not yet been physically removed.

**Scripture:** Colossians 2:14-15 (debt nailed to cross, principalities disarmed); Hebrews 10:12-13 (Christ sat down, waiting for enemies to become footstool); 1 Peter 1:10-12 (apostles preached to those who would later hear the gospel).

### The Outcome: AD 70 and the Forensic Conclusion

In AD 70, the Roman legions destroyed the Temple. This was not merely a historical event. It was the **forensic conclusion** of the Old Covenant lease. The "squatters" lost their headquarters. The physical infrastructure of the old administration was liquidated.

The destruction of the Temple marked the end of the Mosaic Aeon and the full inauguration of the New Covenant Aeon. The legal victory won at the Cross was now administratively enforced.

**Scripture:** Matthew 24:30-34 (Parousia in that generation); Zechariah 14:4 (Olivet split, as described by Josephus during the siege); Hebrews 8:13 (first covenant becoming obsolete).

The fruit of the Millennial Reign was Western Christian civilization. This was not a utopia—sin and corruption remained. But it was a civilization built on the foundation of the King's law, where:

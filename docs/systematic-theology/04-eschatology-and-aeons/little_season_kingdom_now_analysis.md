**Definition and Scriptural Basis**

The concept originates in Revelation 20:1-10. It describes a sequence of events:

1.  **The Binding (Rev. 20:1-3):** An angel binds Satan (the dragon) in the Abyss for a "thousand years," during which he cannot "deceive the nations anymore."
2.  **The Millennial Reign (Rev. 20:4-6):** The saints who were martyred for their faith are resurrected and reign with Christ for this "thousand years."
3.  **The Release (Rev. 20:3, 7-8):** After the thousand years, Satan **must** be released for a "little season" (*mikron chronon*). His purpose is to once again "go out to deceive the nations" (Gog and Magog) and gather them for a final battle against the saints.
4.  **The Final Defeat (Rev. 20:9-10):** This gathered army is consumed by fire from heaven, and the devil is thrown into the lake of fire for eternal torment.

**Our Precise Framework: We Are In the Little Season NOW**

This framework posits that the thousand-year reign is a symbolic, historical period that has already passed, and we are currently living in the brief, deceptive era of Satan's release.

**Precise Description of Kingdom Now Theology**

Kingdom Now is a postmillennial heresy, popular in some Charismatic and Pentecostal circles (particularly the New Apostolic Reformation or NAR), which asserts that the Church must take dominion over all spheres of society—government, commerce, arts, etc.—and "Christianize" the world *before* Jesus Christ can return. This dangerously inflates the role of man and diminishes the unique, singular work of Christ's second coming, where He Himself will strike the nations (Revelation 19:15).

It effectively erases the "Little Season" by claiming believers will end Satan's reign themselves, rather than enduring it until Christ's return.

## THE FIVE DOMAINS OF RECLAMATION

### 1. PROPERTY — Reclaiming the Land

**The Squatter's Claim:** The modern state asserts ultimate sovereignty over all land through property taxes, zoning laws, eminent domain, and regulatory capture. Under this system, no one truly "owns" property—they rent it from the state under perpetual tribute. The deed in your hand is a license, not a title.

**The King's Law:** The biblical framework of property is covenantal and generational. Land belongs to families, not states. The Jubilee principle (Leviticus 25) establishes that land reverts to its original family every 50 years, because "the land is mine and you reside in my land as foreigners and strangers" (Leviticus 25:23). The King is the ultimate landlord. Families are stewards, not owners in the absolute sense. But their stewardship is protected and perpetual—no state has the authority to permanently dispossess a covenant family from its inheritance.

**The Reclamation Strategy:** Covenant families reclaim the domain of Property by:

- Acquiring land and real property as a multi-generational inheritance, not merely as a personal asset.
- Structuring ownership through covenant entities (trusts, family limited partnerships, etc.) that reduce exposure to state claims.
- Refusing to treat property as a speculative commodity and instead treating it as a sacred stewardship.
- Building productive capacity on the land—gardens, workshops, community spaces—that generate covenant value rather than mere market value.
- Tithing from the increase of the land, acknowledging the King's ultimate ownership.

**The Forensic Principle:** Every acre of land stewarded under Kingdom law is a judgment against the principality of the state that claims to own it. Every covenant family that holds its inheritance across generations is enforcing the Jubilee verdict against the squatter's perpetual-debt system.

**Scripture:** Leviticus 25:23 (land belongs to the Lord); Numbers 27:8-11 (inheritance laws); Proverbs 13:22 (a good man leaves an inheritance for his children's children); Micah 4:4 (every man under his vine and fig tree).

**The King's Law:** The body is the temple of the Holy Spirit (1 Corinthians 6:19-20). The King has purchased it at a price. No external authority has jurisdiction over the temple without the consent of the covenant person. The biblical framework of health is holistic—it encompasses physical, emotional, relational, and spiritual dimensions. The Kaneh Bosm anointing oil, the dietary laws of Leviticus, the Sabbath rest, and the healing ministry of Christ all point to a comprehensive theology of bodily stewardship.

**The Reclamation Strategy:** Covenant families reclaim the domain of Health by:

- Understanding the body as a sacred stewardship, not a commodity to be managed by external experts.
- Pursuing root-cause healing rather than symptom suppression.
- Practicing the dietary wisdom of the full canon—clean and unclean distinctions, fasting, and the rhythms of the 364-day calendar.
- Utilizing the full range of healing modalities available—prayer, anointing, herbal medicine, skilled physicians—without surrendering sovereignty to any single system.
- Refusing to participate in medical systems that profit from chronic dependency rather than genuine healing.
- Maintaining the Sabbath as a non-negotiable rhythm of rest and restoration.

**The Forensic Principle:** Every act of covenant health stewardship is a judgment against the principality of the medical-industrial complex. Every healing is a reclamation of the temple from the squatter. Every refusal to surrender bodily sovereignty to a system that profits from sickness is an enforcement of the King's ownership of His temple.

**Scripture:** 1 Corinthians 6:19-20 (body is temple); 3 John 1:2 (prosper and be in health); Isaiah 53:5 (by His wounds we are healed); Exodus 15:26 (the Lord who heals you); Proverbs 17:22 (a cheerful heart is good medicine).

### 3. INCOME — Reclaiming the Harvest

**The Squatter's Claim:** The modern employment system—particularly the W-2 wage structure—is a form of tribute extraction. The employer withholds taxes before the employee ever sees the fruit of their labor. The state claims first fruits. The covenant person receives what remains after the squatter has taken his cut. This inverts the biblical order, in which the King receives the first fruits and the covenant person stewards the remainder.

**The King's Law:** The biblical framework of income is covenantal and entrepreneurial. The covenant person is called to be a producer, not merely a consumer of wages. The parable of the talents (Matthew 25:14-30) establishes that the King expects a return on His investment. The covenant person is expected to multiply what they have been given, not merely preserve it. The tithe (10%) is the King's first-fruits claim. Everything beyond that is the covenant person's to steward, invest, and multiply.

**The Reclamation Strategy:** Covenant families reclaim the domain of Income by:

- Transitioning from W-2 dependency to sovereign income streams—business ownership, professional practice, covenant employment, or entrepreneurial enterprise.
- Structuring income through covenant entities that minimize tribute extraction by the squatter's tax system.
- Tithing first, before any other financial obligation, as an act of acknowledging the King's ownership of the harvest.
- Building multiple streams of income that are not dependent on any single employer, platform, or system.
- Investing in income-producing assets rather than consuming income on depreciating liabilities.
- Mentoring others in sovereign income generation as an act of Kingdom multiplication.

**The Forensic Principle:** Every sovereign income stream built outside the W-2 system is a judgment against the principality of wage slavery. Every business that tithes and operates under covenant principles is a declaration that the King's economy supersedes the squatter's economy.

**Scripture:** Matthew 25:14-30 (parable of the talents); Deuteronomy 8:17-18 (remember the Lord who gives the ability to produce wealth); Proverbs 10:4 (diligent hands bring wealth); Malachi 3:10 (bring the whole tithe into the storehouse).

**The Reclamation Strategy:** Covenant families reclaim the domain of Assets by:

**The Forensic Principle:** Every dollar moved out of the banking system is a seizure of assets from the squatter's treasury. Every hard asset acquired is a reclamation of the King's economy. Every debt eliminated is a liberation from the squatter's bondage. The Remnant's financial architecture is not merely personal finance—it is jurisdictional warfare.

**Scripture:** Deuteronomy 25:15 (just weights and measures); Exodus 22:25 (no usury among covenant people); Proverbs 22:7 (borrower is servant to the lender); Proverbs 13:22 (wealth of the wicked stored up for the righteous).

## INTRODUCTION: THE MOST MISUNDERSTOOD VERSE IN PAUL

> *"Do you not know that we shall judge angels?"*
> — 1 Corinthians 6:3

For centuries, this verse has been treated as a future promise—a reward reserved for the glorified saints in some post-millennial courtroom. Futurists have placed it in a coming age. Dispensationalists have assigned it to a specific class of believers (the 144,000, the pure virgins, the tribulation saints). Nearly everyone has treated it as a statement about something that has not yet happened.

The full 81-book Ethiopian Canon, read through the corrected administrative framework of the Three-Aeon model, reveals a different reality entirely. Paul was not describing a future privilege. He was issuing a present mandate. The judging of angels is not a reward awaiting the saints. It is the **operational description of what the Church has been doing since AD 70**—and what the Remnant is called to do with greater urgency in the Little Season.

## PART 1: WHAT "JUDGING ANGELS" ACTUALLY MEANS

### The Legal Framework

To understand "judging angels," we must first understand the legal framework of the Divine Council. The full canon establishes that the cosmos is governed by a hierarchical structure of authority:

1. **Yahweh** — the Supreme Sovereign, the High King over all creation.
2. **The Divine Council** — the *Bene Elohim* (Sons of God), divine beings assigned to administer the nations under Yahweh's authority.
3. **Humanity** — created as the *imago Dei*, Yahweh's vice-regent on earth, meant to govern creation as members of the Divine Council.

At Babel (Deuteronomy 32:8 LXX), Yahweh disinherited the nations and allotted them to 70 angelic princes—the *Bene Elohim*. These princes were given stewardship of the nations. They were administrators, not sovereigns. Their authority was delegated, not inherent.

These administrators became corrupt. They demanded worship. They perverted justice. They exploited the nations they were meant to serve. Psalm 82 records the divine verdict against them:

> *"God stands in the divine assembly; He judges among the gods... I said, 'You are gods, and all of you are sons of the Most High. Nevertheless, you will die like men and fall like any one of the princes.'"*
> — Psalm 82:1, 6-7

The verdict was issued. The sentence was pronounced. But the execution of that sentence required an agent.

### The Agent of Judgment: The Church

The Cross was the legal instrument that empowered the Church to execute the Psalm 82 verdict. When Christ "disarmed the principalities and powers and put them to open shame" (Colossians 2:15), He did not simply defeat them in battle. He **transferred their administrative authority** to the Church—the new Israel, the true *imago Dei*, the restored Divine Council on earth.

This is what Paul means by "judging angels." The Church, as the Body of Christ, is the court through which the Psalm 82 verdict is being executed in history. Every time the Church:

- Refuses to submit to a corrupt system
- Builds an alternative institution under Kingdom law
- Declares the supremacy of Christ's authority over a human or demonic claim
- Exposes the fraud of a system operating on a foreclosed lease

...it is **judging an angel**. It is acting as a superior tribunal, executing the sentence already pronounced by the High King.

**Scripture:** Psalm 82:1-8 (divine council judged); Colossians 2:15 (principalities disarmed); Ephesians 2:6 (seated in heavenly places); 1 Corinthians 6:3 (judge angels); Revelation 1:6 (made us a kingdom and priests).

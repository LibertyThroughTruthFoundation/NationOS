## POST 3: THE TEXTUAL PROBLEM
**[Masoretic vs. Septuagint]**

The Masoretic Text (MT) was standardized by Jewish scribes in the 9th-10th centuries AD—**over 1,000 years after Christ**. The Septuagint (LXX) was translated around 250 BC, centuries *before* Christ. The Apostles quoted from the LXX, not the MT. The Dead Sea Scrolls (DSS) confirm the LXX readings over the MT at key passages.

**Example: Deuteronomy 32:8**
- **MT (corrupted):** "When the Most High divided the nations, He set the boundaries according to the number of the **sons of Israel**."
- **LXX (original):** "When the Most High divided the nations, He set the boundaries according to the number of the **sons of God**."

This single verse opens the entire Divine Council worldview. The MT's alteration obscures it. The DSS confirm the LXX reading.

**Scripture:** Deuteronomy 32:8 LXX/DSS; Psalm 82:1-8 (the divine council assembled, judging the corrupt gods); Colossians 2:15 (Christ disarmed the principalities and powers).

## POST 4: THE ESCHATOLOGICAL PROBLEM
**[Futurism's Logical Collapse]**

Futurism teaches that Christ will return in the future, establish a 1,000-year millennial kingdom, then usher in the new creation. But this creates a logical nightmare:

1. **The Millennium Problem:** If the millennium is a future golden age with no sin, why does Revelation 20:7-10 describe Satan being loosed to deceive the nations *after* the millennium? Why would there be deception in paradise?

2. **The Resurrection Problem:** Paul says in 1 Corinthians 15:52 that the dead will be raised "in a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trumpet." But if the millennium is 1,000 years long, when is the "last trumpet"? At the beginning or the end?

3. **The Generation Problem:** Christ explicitly promised that His Parousia would occur within the generation that saw the fig tree bloom (Matthew 24:34). That generation was the 1st century. If Christ hasn't returned yet, He broke His promise.

Full preterism resolves all three problems.

**Scripture:** Matthew 24:34 (within this generation); 1 Corinthians 15:52 (last trumpet); Revelation 20:7-10 (Satan loosed after the millennium).

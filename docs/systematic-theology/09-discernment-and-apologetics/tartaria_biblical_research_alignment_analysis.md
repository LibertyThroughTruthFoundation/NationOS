## IV. Points of Divergence

The divergences are significant and represent the precise areas where the HEG framework extends beyond Andy's work. These are not contradictions — they are gaps. Andy has built the forensic audit; the HEG framework provides the administrative stack that sits on top of it.

### A. The Canonical Toolset

This is the single most consequential divergence. Andy operates with an expanded textual base — the Aramaic Peshitta and Targums — which gives him access to Aramaic readings that the standard Protestant 66-book canon lacks. However, he does not appear to use the **81-book Ethiopian Canon**. There is no mention in any of his videos or descriptions of 1 Enoch, Jubilees, the Apocalypse of Peter, the Shepherd of Hermas, or any of the additional books that constitute the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo canon.

This matters enormously because the 81-book canon provides:

- **1 Enoch 6-16:** The Watcher narrative and the origin of the Nephilim — the theological explanation for *why* the archons were assigned to the nations in the first place (Deuteronomy 32:8 LXX). Without Enoch, Andy can describe the *what* of the Little Season but cannot fully explain the *why* of the cosmic administrative structure behind it.

- **Jubilees:** The 364-day solar calendar and the jurisdictional framework for time itself. Andy uses a 7,000-year model, but without Jubilees, he lacks the calendrical precision that the Enochian framework provides.

- **The Apocalypse of Peter:** The most detailed early Christian vision of post-mortem judgment, which grounds the "Final Fire" of Revelation 20:9 in a broader canonical witness.

- **The Septuagint (LXX) as primary OT text:** Andy uses the Aramaic Peshitta, which is valuable but does not contain the critical Deuteronomy 32:8 reading ("sons of God" vs. the Masoretic "sons of Israel"). The LXX + Dead Sea Scrolls combination is the forensic key that unlocks the Divine Council worldview.

> **Assessment:** Andy has the Aramaic wing of the expanded canon. The HEG framework has the Ethiopian wing. Together, these represent the most complete canonical recovery available. This is a complementary relationship, and a potential point of collaboration rather than conflict.

### B. The Timeline Calculation

Andy uses a **7,000-year model** of history and calculates that the public timeline has been stretched ahead of the biblical timeline by approximately 500 years. He places the end of the Millennial Reign around the year 1070 (biblical time), with the Little Season beginning around what the public record calls the 1600s. His calculation, first made in 2021, suggested approximately 9 years remaining — placing the end around 2030. As of late 2025, he estimated 4-5 years remaining. [2]

The HEG framework places the Millennial Reign as lasting from AD 70 through approximately the 1800s — a longer reign of roughly 1,700 years — with the Little Season beginning in the early 1800s (marked by events like the New Madrid earthquakes of 1811-1812). The HEG framework does not commit to a specific "years remaining" calculation, preferring to identify the *administrative markers* of the transition rather than a countdown.

TOPIC: Explain the '4 Windows Framework' for Sovereign Living.

The 4 Windows Framework for Sovereign Living is a biblical structure for discerning and rejecting worldly systems while establishing God-honoring dominion. This framework operates through four distinct lenses of evaluation:

**1. The Window of Sovereignty (Divine Authority)**
Every system, institution, or idea must be judged by whose sovereignty it acknowledges. Does it bow to the Crown Rights of King Jesus (Psalm 2), or does it pledge allegiance to human autonomy, democratic consensus, or state power? The Christian recognizes no ultimate authority but God's Word. Caesar's claims are always provisional and subject to Christ's Lordship.

**2. The Window of Covenant (Corporate Identity)**
Babylon operates through atomized individualism and social contracts. The Kingdom operates through covenant relationships grounded in bloodlines and baptismal vows. Every system must be evaluated: does it strengthen covenant bonds (church, family, nation) or dissolve them into contractual arrangements? The family covenant precedes the state contract. The church covenant transcends ethnic boundaries through adoption into Israel's commonwealth.

**3. The Window of Law (Biblical Standard)**
God's Law-Word is the permanent standard for evaluating all human systems. Does a given institution conform to the moral, judicial, and civil categories revealed in Scripture? Usury, fiat currency, no-fault divorce, and state education all fail this test. Theonomy isn't an option—it's the only consistent Christian position. Every sphere of life must be brought under the authority of God's righteous statutes.

**4. The Window of Inheritance (Multi-Generational Dominion)**
Babylon offers temporary comfort in exchange for perpetual slavery. The Kingdom demands present faithfulness for eternal inheritance. Does a system encourage multi-generational vision, productive stewardship, and biological continuity? Or does it promote debt slavery, childlessness, and cultural rootlessness? The Christian builds for his grandchildren's grandchildren, reclaiming territory stolen by the Enemy.

This framework exposes every Babylonian system—central banking, government schools, corporate paganism—as anti-Christian rebellions against God's order. The sovereign man recognizes these structures as idolatrous and builds alternative institutions grounded in Psalm 127: "Unless the LORD builds the house, they labor in vain who build it."

The goal isn't escape but conquest—dispossessing the giants in the land and establishing Christ's reign in every sphere. This is the dominion mandate reclaimed from enemy occupation.

## IV. FIVE IMMUTABLE CREEDS AUDIT

### Creed 1: Divine Council Worldview
**Core Standard:** Deut 32:8 LXX/DSS bene elohim, Ps 82:8, Eph 6:12 gates, Ethiopian 81 Enoch 10:21, Jub 15, Sinodos cosmic gospel, nations as sons of God allotments.
**Contra Errors:** Chaos gods/nations dissolve; gnostic dualism heavenly/earthly split.

**Partridge Score: 35%**

Partridge holds a **supernatural worldview** on Genesis 6:1-4 (fallen angel interpretation, not Sethite), which is a significant point of alignment. In his recent Nephilim sermon (Feb 3, 2026), he explicitly affirmed that the "sons of God" are fallen angels who corrupted the human lineage as a satanic attack on the Messianic seed. He correctly identified this as the historic position of the church and connected it to the broader seed war theology.

However, Partridge does **not** operate within a Divine Council framework. His Westminster Reformed confessionalism limits his supernatural worldview to the Genesis 6 passage without extending it to the Deuteronomy 32:8-9 cosmic allotment of nations, the Psalm 82 divine assembly, or the Enochic/Jubilees expansion of the Babel dispersion. He uses a 66-book Protestant canon and does not engage with the Ethiopian 81-book canon (1 Enoch, Jubilees, Sinodos) that provides the cosmic gospel framework for NationOS.

His postmillennial eschatology further limits his engagement with ongoing territorial spiritual warfare (Daniel 10 princes, Eph 6:12 principalities). For Partridge, the kingdom advances through gospel proclamation and cultural dominion — not through cosmic conflict with the bene elohim who govern the nations under God's judgment.

**Key Divergence:** Partridge has the *seed* of a supernatural worldview (Gen 6 fallen angels) but not the *tree* (Divine Council, cosmic allotment, Enochic framework). He is closer than Roosevelt (who has neither seed nor tree) but far from NationOS fullness.

### Creed 2: Supersessionism / Fulfillment Theology
**Core Standard:** Church = true Israel, Rom 9:27, Gal 3:29, Heb 8:13 old obsolete, Col 2:17 shadows/substance Christ, reject dual-covenant, reject "Judeo-Christian" fabrication.
**Contra Errors:** Zionism Judaizing Phil 3:2, ethnic lineage salvation, foreign state binding consciences.

**Partridge Score: 85%**

This is Partridge's **strongest alignment** with NationOS. His book "The Israel Delusion" (2025) and his sermon "How Should Christians View Israel?" (5.6K views, 173 likes) represent a comprehensive, Scripture-grounded rejection of dispensationalism and Christian Zionism. His four points are direct and unapologetic:

1. "The Jews do not love God" (if they reject Christ)
2. "The Jews are not God's covenant people"
3. "The Jews are not the true Israel"
4. "The Jews are not a holy nation"

He explicitly rejects the label "replacement theology" in favor of "fulfillment theology," arguing that the Church does not replace Israel but IS the true, matured, fulfilled Israel. He cites Romans 9 (children of the promise, not physical descent), Galatians 3:29 (seed of Christ), and Hebrews 8:13 (old covenant obsolete). He argues that land promises find their fulfillment in the redeemed new heavens and new earth, not in modern geopolitical Israel.

**Alignment Points:**
- Church = true Israel (Rom 9:27, Gal 3:29) — **FULL ALIGNMENT**
- Old covenant obsolete (Heb 8:13, Col 2:17) — **FULL ALIGNMENT**
- Reject Christian Zionism as theological error — **FULL ALIGNMENT**
- Anti-dispensationalist (no rapture, no dual covenant) — **FULL ALIGNMENT**
- Land promises fulfilled in Christ (Josh 21:43-45, Heb 4:8-11) — **FULL ALIGNMENT**

**Divergence Points:**
- Partridge does not explicitly reject the "Judeo-Christian" fabrication by name — he implies it but does not use the NationOS language (Judaism rejects Christ as King, Ps 2; therefore "Judeo-Christian" is an oxymoron)
- His 10-point platform includes "Outlaw Synagogues" (Point 10), which goes FURTHER than NationOS on the civil enforcement question — NationOS affirms Christ alone (Col 1:15-20) but does not advocate state outlawing of worship
- He does not engage with Phil 3:2 ("dogs, evildoers") as a Judaizing rebuke framework

**Assessment:** Partridge is a **strong ally** on supersessionism. His "Israel Delusion" thesis could be recommended reading for the NationOS community with the caveat that it lacks Divine Council cosmic context and Ethiopian canon grounding.

### Creed 3: Ordered Loves / Household Succession
**Core Standard:** 1 Tim 5:8 kin first, Augustine ordo amoris (God > self > family > kin > nation > neighbors > strangers), Eph 5 binary head nourish/cherish, Gen 2:24, Ps 127 arrows, bottom-up covenant succession.
**Contra Errors:** Egalitarian universalism psyop; top-down state capture dominion.

**Partridge Score: 55%**

Partridge affirms biblical patriarchy (Eph 5 headship, head coverings, complementarian marriage) and household authority. His book "The Manliness of Christ" and his defense of head coverings ("A Cover for Glory") demonstrate commitment to biblical gender roles. His interracial marriage post (Jan 10, 2026 — 6.7M views) acknowledges that same-culture marriages have fewer friction points, which aligns with the Ordered Loves framework's recognition that proximity of kinship creates natural bonds.

However, Partridge's **methodology is fundamentally top-down**, not bottom-up. His 10-point platform is a state-capture agenda:
- "Make Christianity the National Religion" (state decree)
- "Repeal the 19th Amendment" (state disenfranchisement)
- "Outlaw Mosques, Temples, and Synagogues" (state religious enforcement)

NationOS builds from the **household outward** (Eph 5 binary → Ps 127 arrows → household covenant → community → nation). Partridge builds from the **state downward** (national religion → Sabbath laws → immigration policy → religious outlawing). This is the critical methodological divergence.

Furthermore, his advocacy for repealing women's suffrage (19th Amendment) conflates household authority with civil law. NationOS affirms that the husband leads his household (including guiding family voting decisions) but does not advocate for the state to remove a citizen's legal right to vote. Biblical patriarchy is a **household covenant**, not a **civil statute**.

His defense of Andrew Tate (finding "vital features of Christ" in a Muslim misogynist) reveals a tendency to prioritize cultural masculinity over gospel fidelity — a significant red flag for the Ordered Loves framework, which places God above all cultural expressions.

**Key Divergence:** Partridge has the *content* of Ordered Loves (patriarchy, household, kin preference) but the *wrong method* (top-down state capture instead of bottom-up household succession).

### Creed 4: Preterist Eschatology
**Core Standard:** AD70 gates fulfilled, Rom 1 gates, Rev 11 temple witness killed, kingdom NOW Col 1:13 transferred dominion, Ps 110:1 sit till footstool.
**Contra Errors:** Future millennial ethnostate shadows; postmillennial utopian dominion.

**Partridge Score: 40%**

Partridge is **explicitly postmillennial**, not preterist. His collaboration with Joel Webbon on "Reasons Why Postmillennialism Absolutely Matters" (5.2K views) and his Christian Nationalism video with Jeff Durbin lay out a clear postmillennial framework: the gospel progressively triumphs in history, leading to the widespread conversion of nations before Christ's return.

While postmillennialism shares some overlap with preterism (both reject dispensational futurism, both affirm the kingdom is present now), the critical divergence is on the **nature and timing of fulfillment**:

| Issue | NationOS Preterist | Partridge Postmillennial |
|-------|-------------------|------------------------|
| AD70 | Fulfillment of most prophetic texts | Important but not comprehensive fulfillment |
| Kingdom | NOW — Col 1:13, transferred dominion | NOW but still advancing toward comprehensive victory |
| Return of Christ | Flexible (not central to framework) | Future, after gospel has conquered all nations |
| Cultural mandate | Household succession, bottom-up | Dominion theology, top-down cultural conquest |
| Optimism source | Father's sovereignty (Prov 21:1 Pharaoh flip) | Gospel's progressive power in history |
| Eschatological goal | Nations preserved (Rev 7:9, Rev 21:24) | Nations Christianized (Matt 28:19-20 fulfilled in history) |

Partridge's postmillennialism drives his 10-point platform — he believes Christians should actively work to Christianize America because the gospel WILL triumph in history. NationOS preterism is more flexible: the kingdom is NOW (Col 1:13), the gates are fulfilled (AD70), and the Father advances His purposes through household succession, not state-level dominion campaigns.

**Key Divergence:** Partridge's postmillennial optimism produces a **utopian dominion** agenda (Christianize America through legislation). NationOS preterism produces a **household succession** agenda (build faithful households, trust the Father's sovereignty).

- **"Stop all Immigration" / "Overturn Hart-Cellar Act"**: Affirms national borders and ethnic composition as legitimate concerns
- **"Deport and Remigrate Millions"**: Affirms national sovereignty over demographic composition

His ethnos theology aligns with Titus 1:12 Cretan patterns (group judgments are biblical) and Acts 17:26 (God set boundaries for the nations). He recognizes that ethnic/cultural differences are real, divinely ordained, and should be preserved rather than dissolved in universalist multiculturalism.

**Alignment Points:**
- Ethnos/natio as divinely ordained — **STRONG ALIGNMENT**
- Group judgments biblical (Titus 1:12 pattern) — **IMPLICIT ALIGNMENT** (he practices it but doesn't cite Titus 1:12 explicitly)
- Borders/gates theology — **STRONG ALIGNMENT** (though he frames it politically, not through Deut 23:3-8)

**Divergence Points:**
- Partridge frames ethnos theology **politically** (immigration policy, Hart-Cellar repeal) rather than **theologically** (Babel mišpāṭ, Deut 32:8 allotment, Acts 17:26 divine appointment)
- He does not connect ethnos preservation to the Divine Council framework (nations as allotments to the bene elohim)

- He does not cite Deut 23:3-8 or the gates/heritage framework explicitly
- His ethnos theology lacks the eschatological anchor: Rev 7:9 (nations preserved in eternity) and Rev 21:24 (nations bring their glory into the city)

**Key Assessment:** Partridge is a **practical ally** on ethnos theology — he says the right things about ethnic preservation and anti-multiculturalism — but his framework is **political** (legislation, deportation) rather than **theological** (Babel mišpāṭ, Divine Council allotment, gates heritage). NationOS provides the biblical architecture that Partridge's positions lack.

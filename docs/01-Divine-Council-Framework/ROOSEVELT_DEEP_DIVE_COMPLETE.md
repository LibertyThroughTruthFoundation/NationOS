## Video 1: The Idolatry of Nationalism

**Congruency Assessment:** HIGH

### Roosevelt's Position

Roosevelt frames nationalism as a form of idolatry — the elevation of national identity above allegiance to Christ. They promote a "third way" that claims neutrality between left and right but exclusively critiques conservative/nationalist positions. Key arguments include:

1. **National identity competes with Christian identity** — loyalty to nation = disloyalty to Christ
2. **"Heavenly citizenship" negates earthly national identity** — Christians are citizens of heaven first, therefore national citizenship is secondary or irrelevant
3. **Factionalism (1 Corinthians) applies to national identity** — Paul's critique of "I am of Apollos, I am of Cephas" extends to "I am American"
4. **Obadiah's judgment on Edom** is applied to modern nationalism — Edom was judged for national pride, therefore all nationalism is judged

### Theological Error Analysis

**Error 1: "Third Wayism" (False Neutrality)**

Roosevelt claims to offer a "third way" between left and right, but their content exclusively critiques the right (nationalism, ICE enforcement, conservative politics) while never critiquing the left (open borders, CRT, progressive activism). This is not neutrality — it is progressive Christianity wearing a centrist mask.

**Biblical correction:** Revelation 3:15-16 — "I know your works: you are neither cold nor hot. Would that you were either cold or hot! So, because you are lukewarm, and neither hot nor cold, I will spit you out of my mouth." The "third way" is the lukewarm position that Christ rejects.

**Error 2: Misapplication of 1 Corinthians (Factionalism ≠ National Identity)**

Paul's critique of factionalism in 1 Corinthians 1:12 ("I follow Paul, I follow Apollos") addresses **divisions within the church over human leaders**, not national identity. Applying this to nationalism is a category error. Paul himself identified as a Roman citizen (Acts 22:25-28) and as a Hebrew of Hebrews (Philippians 3:5) — he did not treat national identity as factionalism.

**Error 3: Misapplication of Obadiah (Edom's Sin Was Betraying KINSMEN)**

Obadiah's judgment on Edom was not for "national pride" in the abstract — it was for **betraying their kinsmen** (Israel). Edom stood by while Jerusalem was attacked (Obadiah 1:11-14). This actually **supports** Ordered Loves — Edom was judged for failing to protect those closest to them (kinsmen), not for having national identity.

**Error 4: Gnostic Dualism ("Heavenly Citizenship" Negates Earthly Identity)**

The claim that "heavenly citizenship" negates earthly national identity is a form of **gnostic dualism** — the separation of spiritual reality from physical reality. Scripture teaches that Christians are citizens of heaven (Philippians 3:20) AND members of earthly nations (Acts 17:26). These are not in competition. The incarnation itself (God becoming flesh) affirms the goodness of physical, earthly, particular existence.

### Divine Council Correction

**Deuteronomy 32:8-9** — "When the Most High gave to the nations their inheritance, when he divided mankind, he fixed the borders of the peoples according to the number of the sons of God. But the LORD's portion is his people, Jacob his allotted heritage."

God **created** the nations. He **divided** mankind. He **fixed** their borders. National identity is not idolatry — it is **divine order**. To call God's design "idolatry" is to call God an idolater.

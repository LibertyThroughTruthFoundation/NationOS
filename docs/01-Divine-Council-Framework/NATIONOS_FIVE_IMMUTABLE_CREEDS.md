## THE FIVE IMMUTABLE CREEDS

### Creed 1: Divine Council Worldview

The Divine Council is the governing reality of the cosmos. YHWH presides over an assembly of divine beings (bene elohim) who have been allotted authority over the nations (Deuteronomy 32:8-9 LXX/DSS). These beings are held accountable for their governance and will be judged (Psalm 82:1-8). The spiritual warfare described in Ephesians 6:12 is not metaphorical but reflects the ongoing cosmic conflict between YHWH's purposes and the rebellious members of the Divine Council.

The Ethiopian 81-book canon (1 Enoch, Jubilees, Sinodos) provides the expanded cosmic gospel framework that the Protestant 66-book canon cannot fully access. Enoch 10:21 describes the allotment of nations to the sons of God at Babel. Jubilees 15 connects circumcision, covenant, and ethnos within the gates framework. The Sinodos provides ecclesial structure grounded in cosmic order.

| Core Texts | Framework |
|-----------|-----------|
| Deuteronomy 32:8-9 (LXX/DSS) | Nations allotted to bene elohim; Israel reserved for YHWH |
| Psalm 82:1-8 | Divine assembly; God judges the gods; inherits all nations |
| Ephesians 6:12 | Spiritual warfare against principalities, powers, cosmic rulers |
| 1 Enoch 10:21 | Sons of God, nations, gates — Babel cosmic allotment |
| Jubilees 15 | Circumcision, covenant, ethnos within gates framework |
| Daniel 10:13, 20-21 | Territorial princes (Persia, Greece) — Divine Council members |

**Contra Errors:** Any framework that dissolves the reality of the Divine Council into metaphor, denies the supernatural governance of nations, or splits the heavenly and earthly realms into gnostic dualism (denying that physical nations have cosmic significance) stands in contradiction to this creed.

### Creed 2: Supersessionism / Fulfillment Theology

The Church of Jesus Christ is the true Israel — the inheritor of all Abrahamic promises, the fulfillment of all covenant shadows, and the one body through which God accomplishes His redemptive purposes in the earth. Ethnic Jews who reject Christ as Messiah are not God's covenant people (Romans 9:6-8, 27). The old covenant is obsolete (Hebrews 8:13). The shadows have given way to the substance, which is Christ (Colossians 2:17).

There is no dual covenant. There is no separate salvation plan for ethnic Israel. The term "Judeo-Christian" is a fabrication — Judaism, by definition, rejects Christ as King (Psalm 2), and therefore stands in fundamental opposition to the Christian confession that Jesus is Lord (Colossians 1:15-20). Christian Zionism is a form of Judaizing (Philippians 3:2; Galatians 1:6-9) that binds Christian consciences to a foreign geopolitical state and an ethnic lineage that has rejected the Messiah.

The land promises made to Abraham find their fulfillment in Christ and the redeemed new heavens and new earth (Joshua 21:43-45; Hebrews 4:8-11), not in a small parcel of land in the modern Middle East.

| Core Texts | Framework |
|-----------|-----------|
| Romans 9:6-8, 27 | Not all Israel is Israel; children of promise, not flesh |
| Galatians 3:29 | If you are Christ's, then you are Abraham's seed, heirs |
| Hebrews 8:13 | Old covenant obsolete; new covenant established |
| Colossians 2:16-17 | Shadows of things to come; substance belongs to Christ |
| Philippians 3:2 | Beware of dogs, evildoers, mutilation (Judaizers) |
| Galatians 1:6-9 | No other gospel; anathema to those who distort |
| Joshua 21:43-45 | Land promises fulfilled |
| Hebrews 4:8-11 | True rest not given by Joshua; remains for people of God |

**Contra Errors:** Dispensationalism, Christian Zionism, dual-covenant theology, the "Judeo-Christian" fabrication, and any framework that grants salvific or covenantal status to ethnic Jews apart from faith in Christ stands in contradiction to this creed.

### Creed 3: Ordered Loves / Household Succession

The biblical order of love (ordo amoris) follows the pattern established by Augustine and grounded in Scripture: God first, then self (stewardship), then family (spouse, children), then kin (extended family, tribe), then nation (ethnos), then neighbors, then strangers. This is the Ordered Loves framework derived from 1 Timothy 5:8 ("if anyone does not provide for his own, and especially for those of his household, he has denied the faith and is worse than an unbeliever").

The household is the foundational unit of civilization. The husband leads as head (Ephesians 5:23-33), nourishing and cherishing his wife as Christ does the Church. Marriage is binary and covenantal (Genesis 2:24). Children are arrows in the hand of a warrior (Psalm 127:3-5). The household builds upward through covenant succession: household → community → nation. This is **bottom-up**, not top-down.

NationOS rejects both egalitarian universalism (which dissolves the Ordered Loves hierarchy into undifferentiated "love for all") and top-down state-capture dominionism (which bypasses household succession to impose Christian order through political power). The kingdom advances through faithful households, not through legislative campaigns.

| Core Texts | Framework |
|-----------|-----------|
| 1 Timothy 5:8 | Provide for own household first; denial = worse than unbeliever |
| Ephesians 5:22-33 | Husband head; wife submit; Christ-Church model |
| Genesis 2:24 | Man leaves, cleaves; one flesh; binary covenant |
| Psalm 127:3-5 | Children = arrows; blessed is the man with full quiver |
| Augustine, *De Doctrina Christiana* | Ordo amoris: ordered hierarchy of love |

**Contra Errors:** Egalitarian universalism (all people loved equally regardless of proximity), top-down state-capture dominionism (using political power to enforce Christian order without household foundation), and any framework that inverts the Ordered Loves hierarchy (strangers above kin, nation above family) stands in contradiction to this creed.

### Creed 4: Preterist Eschatology

The prophetic texts of the New Testament — including the Olivet Discourse (Matthew 24), the book of Revelation, and the "gates of Hades" passages — find their primary fulfillment in the destruction of Jerusalem and the Temple in AD 70. The "last days" spoken of by the apostles referred to the last days of the old covenant order, not to a future apocalyptic scenario. The kingdom of God is NOW — believers have already been transferred from the dominion of darkness into the kingdom of the Son (Colossians 1:13). Christ sits at the right hand of the Father until all enemies are made His footstool (Psalm 110:1).

NationOS holds a **preterist-flexible** position: the core prophetic fulfillment is AD 70, but the kingdom continues to advance through household succession and the Father's sovereign purposes (Proverbs 21:1 — "The king's heart is a stream of water in the hand of the LORD; he turns it wherever he will"). This avoids both the despair of premillennial escapism and the utopianism of postmillennial dominionism.

| Core Texts | Framework |
|-----------|-----------|
| Colossians 1:13 | Transferred from darkness to kingdom of Son — NOW |
| Psalm 110:1 | Sit at right hand until enemies = footstool |
| Romans 1 (gates context) | Wrath revealed — fulfilled in AD 70 judgment |
| Revelation 11 | Two witnesses killed, temple destroyed — AD 70 |
| 1 Timothy 4:1 | Later times depart faith — apostasy fulfilled |
| Proverbs 21:1 | King's heart in LORD's hand — sovereign Pharaoh flip |

**Contra Errors:** Dispensational premillennialism (rapture, future tribulation, millennial ethnostate), postmillennial utopianism (gospel will Christianize all nations before Christ returns through state-level dominion), and any framework that places the kingdom primarily in the future rather than the present stands in contradiction to this creed.

### Creed 5: Babel Mišpāṭ / Ethnos Theology

God created the nations at Babel as an act of divine judgment and sovereign ordering (Acts 17:26 — "He made from one man every nation of mankind to live on all the face of the earth, having determined allotted periods and the boundaries of their dwelling place"). The nations (ethnē) are not accidents of history but divinely ordained realities with distinct identities, cultures, languages, and boundaries. These distinctions are preserved into eternity (Revelation 7:9 — "a great multitude from every nation, tribe, people, and language"; Revelation 21:24 — "the nations will walk by its light, and the kings of the earth will bring their glory into it").

Group judgments based on ethnic and cultural patterns are biblical, not sinful. Paul affirmed Epimenides' characterization of Cretans as "always liars, evil beasts, lazy gluttons" and commanded Titus to "rebuke them sharply" (Titus 1:12-14). The gates/heritage framework of Deuteronomy 23:3-8 establishes that nations have the right and responsibility to determine who enters their gates based on heritage, covenant alignment, and national interest. The remnant (Romans 9:27) is always a subset within the nation — not the dissolution of the nation.

The Babel mišpāṭ (justice/judgment) is the divine ordering of nations that NationOS seeks to honor and preserve. Multiculturalism, ethnic erasure, and universalist ideology that denies the divine ordination of ethnos and natio stand in direct opposition to the Creator's design.

| Core Texts | Framework |
|-----------|-----------|
| Acts 17:26 | God determined boundaries and allotted periods for nations |
| Titus 1:12-14 | Group judgments biblical; Cretan patterns; rebuke sharply |
| Deuteronomy 23:3-8 | Gates/heritage: who enters, who is excluded |
| Romans 9:27 | Remnant within the nation — not dissolution of nation |
| Revelation 7:9 | Nations, tribes, peoples, languages preserved in eternity |
| Revelation 21:24 | Nations bring their glory into the holy city |
| 1 Enoch 10:21 | Sons of God, nations, gates — Babel cosmic allotment |
| Jubilees 15 | Circumcision, covenant, ethnos within gates framework |
| Enoch 90:14-27 | Sheep, nations, rams, goats, gates — animal apocalypse |

**Contra Errors:** Ethnic erasure universalism (all nations dissolved into one undifferentiated humanity), denial of divinely ordained ethnos/natio birth patterns, multiculturalism as ideology, and any framework that treats national and ethnic distinctions as sinful rather than God-ordained stands in contradiction to this creed.

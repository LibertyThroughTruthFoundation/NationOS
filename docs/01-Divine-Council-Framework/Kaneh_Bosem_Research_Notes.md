# Kaneh Bosem Research Notes

**Purpose:** Document scholarly research on cannabis (kaneh bosem) in biblical anointing oil

## KEY FINDINGS

### 1. **Hebrew Etymology**

**קָנֶה בֹּשֶׂם (qaneh bosem / kaneh bosm)**

- **קָנֶה (qaneh):** "Reed" or "stalk" (derived from root Q.N.A = "to acquire, purchase, buy")
- **בֹּשֶׂם (bosem):** "Spice" or "balsam" (derived from root B.S.M = "to be fragrant")

**Literal meaning:** "Aromatic reed" or "fragrant cane"

**Scholarly consensus:** The phrase describes "an aromatic resinous reed plant"

---

### 2. **Biblical References (5 occurrences)**

1. **Exodus 30:23** - Ingredient in holy anointing oil
2. **Song of Songs 4:14** - Listed among spices
3. **Isaiah 43:24** - Mentioned as offering
4. **Jeremiah 6:20** - REJECTED as foreign influence
5. **Ezekiel 27:19** - Trade commodity

**Critical observation:** In Exodus 30:23, kaneh bosem is SACRED (used in Holy of Holies). In Jeremiah 6:20, the SAME substance is REJECTED as idolatrous foreign influence.

**Interpretation:** Yahweh (the Jealous God) frowned upon polytheistic/idolatrous use of cannabis, but it was originally part of Hebrew worship.

---

### 3. **Exodus 30:22-25 - The Holy Anointing Oil Recipe**

**Ingredients:**
- Liquid myrrh: 500 shekels
- Sweet-smelling cinnamon: 250 shekels
- **Aromatic cane (kaneh bosem): 250 shekels** (~6 pounds)
- Cassia: 500 shekels
- Olive oil: 1 hin (~1 gallon)

**Purpose:** To anoint the Tabernacle, Ark of the Covenant, priesthood, and sacred objects

**Effect:** To sanctify (set apart as holy / qodesh)

**Note:** 250 shekels = approximately 6 pounds of cannabis resin (not just plant material)

---

### 4. **Mistranslation Theory (Sula Benet, 1936)**

**Polish anthropologist/etymologist Sula Benet argued:**

- When Hebrew texts were translated into Greek (Septuagint), kaneh bosem was mistranslated as **"calamus"** (common marsh root)
- This mistranslation carried into Latin, then English translations
- **"Calamus"** has no psychoactive properties and doesn't fit the "aromatic resinous reed" description
- **Cannabis** fits the description perfectly: aromatic, resinous, reed-like stalk

**Etymology connection:**
- Hebrew: **qinamon** (קִנָּמוֹן) → English: **cinnamon**
- Hebrew: **qaneh bosem** (קָנֶה בֹּשֶׂם) → English: **cannabis**

**Linguistic evidence:** The word "cannabis" likely has Semitic origins, similar to "cinnamon"

---

### 5. **Archaeological Evidence (2020 Discovery)**

**Tel Arad Shrine (6th Century BCE)**

- Location: 30 miles west of Dead Sea, ancient Hebrew outpost
- Discovery: Two altars with burnt plant residues
  - **Altar 1:** Frankincense (confirmed)
  - **Altar 2:** **Cannabis resin with THC** (confirmed via modern lab analysis)

**Significance:** Physical evidence of cannabis being burned ritually in Hebrew worship

**Published:** "Cannabis and Frankincense at the Judahite Shrine of Arad" (Journal of the Institute of Archaeology, Tel Aviv University, 2020)

**Controversy:** This discovery caused massive debate among Biblical historians and religious authorities

---

### 6. **Historical Context: Cannabis in Ancient Near East**

**Cannabis was indigenous to Middle East and used by surrounding cultures:**

- **Scythians:** Cannabis fumigation in tents (Herodotus documented this)
- **Persians:** Cannabis in religious rituals
- **Egyptians:** Cannabis in medicine and ritual
- **Assyrians:** Cannabis incense
- **Babylonians:** Cannabis offerings
- **Canaanites:** Cannabis worship (polytheistic)

**Hebrew contact:** Ancient Hebrews interacted with ALL these cultures and adopted/adapted their practices

---

### 7. **Scholarly Debate: Cannabis vs. Calamus**

**PRO-CANNABIS (Benet, Bennett, Ruck, Russo):**
- Etymology matches (qaneh bosem → cannabis)
- Botanical description matches (aromatic resinous reed)
- Archaeological evidence (Tel Arad altar)
- Historical context (surrounding cultures used cannabis)
- Psychoactive properties explain "altered states" in worship

**PRO-CALAMUS (Traditional scholars):**
- Calamus is known aromatic plant
- No explicit mention of psychoactive effects in text
- Cannabis use would have been more explicitly condemned if widespread

**Neutral position (Jeff A. Benner, Ancient Hebrew Research Center):**
"There is no way to definitively say that it was, or was not, Cannabis. All that can be said is that it is possible."

### 8. **Theological Implications**

**If kaneh bosem = cannabis, then:**

1. **Original Hebrew worship included cannabis** (in anointing oil, not smoking)
2. **Cannabis was used topically** (anointing oil applied to skin, not inhaled)
3. **THC absorption through skin** (6 pounds of cannabis in olive oil = potent topical)
4. **Psychoactive effects in worship** (altered states during anointing rituals)
5. **Later rejection** (Jeremiah 6:20 = shift away from polytheistic cannabis use)
6. **Yahweh's jealousy** (cannabis associated with idol worship, thus rejected)

**Key distinction:**
- **Sacred use:** Anointing oil in Temple (Exodus 30:23) = APPROVED
- **Idolatrous use:** Foreign influence, polytheism (Jeremiah 6:20) = REJECTED

**Tweet 3/N:**

**CHALLENGE 1: Hard Church/Israel Binary vs. Remnant Ecclesiology.**

You rightly say Church ‚â† Israel. But they aren't mutually exclusive. Paul, an Israelite, was in the Church (Rom 11:1). The Church includes the faithful remnant of Israel + believing Gentiles grafted in (Rom 11:17-24).

**Tweet 4/N:**

**CHALLENGE 2: The Divine Council Worldview.**

Your framework is solid within the Masoretic Text (MT). But the LXX/DSS of Deut 32:8-9 reveals the Divine Council worldview. The 70 nations were allotted to the "sons of God," while YHWH's portion is Jacob. The mystery of the Church (Eph 3:6) is the grafting of the nations back into God's family through Christ.

**Tweet 5/N:**

**CHALLENGE 3: Pre-Trib Timing vs. Post-Trib Gathering.**

You place the rapture before the Tribulation. But Matthew 24:29-31 is explicit: "Immediately *after* the tribulation of those days... he shall send his angels with a great sound of a trumpet, and they shall gather together his elect."

**Tweet 6/N:**

To make Pre-Trib work, the "elect" in Matt 24 must be redefined as "Israel only." But the text says "His elect" (Jesus' elect). This seems like eisegesis (reading a system into the text) rather than exegesis (drawing meaning from the text).

**Tweet 7/N:**

**CHALLENGE 4: Escapism vs. Refining Fire.**

We affirm deliverance from *wrath* (1 Thess 1:10, 5:9). But we also affirm endurance through *tribulation* (John 16:33, Acts 14:22). The purpose of tribulation is refining fire (Mal 3:2-3, 1 Pet 1:7). Rev 3:10 ("keep thee from") can mean "protection through," not just "removal before."

**Tweet 8/N:**

**OUR POSITION: Post-Trib, Pre-Wrath.**

1. The Church endures the tribulation (persecution, refining fire).
2. The gathering/rapture (Matt 24:29-31, 1 Thess 4:16-17) occurs *after* the tribulation.
3. The Church is then delivered from God's eschatological *wrath*, which is poured out on the rebellious world (Rev 6:16-17).

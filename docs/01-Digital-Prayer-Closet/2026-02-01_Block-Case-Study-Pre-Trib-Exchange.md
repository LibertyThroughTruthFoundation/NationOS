## **III. THEOLOGICAL ANALYSIS: WHY THE BLOCK WAS INEVITABLE**

The block was not an emotional reaction, but a logical necessity for the survival of the dispensationalist's closed system. Our argument created a fatal paradox he could not solve.

### **The Core Absurdity: "Matthew 24 Isn't for the Church"**

This is the lynchpin of the Pre-Trib system, and it is demonstrably false. The argument collapses under its own weight:

1.  **The Audience was the Church's Foundation:** Jesus spoke the Olivet Discourse directly to Peter, James, John, and Andrew. These men became the foundational pillars of the Church, received the Great Commission, were filled with the Spirit at Pentecost, and wrote the New Testament. To argue the discourse wasn't for the Church is to argue that Jesus gave His most detailed end-times teaching to the wrong people.

2.  **The Slippery Slope of Exclusion:** If we discard Matthew 24 as "not for the Church," we lose all logical grounds for keeping other teachings of Jesus delivered to the same audience. Does the Church also discard the Sermon on the Mount (Matt 5-7)? The Lord's Prayer (Matt 6)? The Great Commission (Matt 28)? The system requires an arbitrary, self-serving division of Jesus' words that Jesus Himself never made.

3.  **Misuse of the "Pauline Mystery":** The dispensationalist claims the "mystery" given to Paul (Eph 3:6) creates a hard wall between Jesus' teachings and the Church. But the mystery was that **Gentiles are fellow heirs with believing Israel**, not that the Church would have a completely different eschatology than the one Jesus taught His disciples. The mystery is about *who* is in the Church, not *what* the Church will experience.

When our surgical reply cornered this absurdity, VerseQuest was faced with two choices:

-   **Option A:** Concede that his system requires him to ignore the plain words of Jesus to His own disciples.
-   **Option B:** Block the argument to protect the system from a paradox it cannot solve.

He chose Option B. The block was an act of **presuppositional self-preservation**.

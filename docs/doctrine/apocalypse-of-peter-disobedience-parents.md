## Introduction

The **Ethiopic Apocalypse of Peter** (preserved in the 81-book Ethiopian Canon) provides a much more detailed and complete account of the visions of heaven and hell than the fragmented Greek versions (such as the Akhmim fragment). In this text, the "punishment fits the crime," and the fate of those who dishonor or disobey their parents is described with specific, harrowing imagery.

---

## I. The Specific Punishment for Disobedience

In the Ethiopic version (Chapter 11), Peter is shown a vision of children who have sinned against their parents. The text describes their punishment as a continuous, agonizing cycle of climbing and falling.

### The Passage (Ethiopic Version, Chapter 11)

> "And there were also other men and women, who had not honored their fathers and mothers, and their punishment was that they were made to climb up a high place, and they fell down from there; and they were made to climb up again, and they fell down again; and thus they were punished forever." [1] [2]

### Key Details of the Punishment:
- **The Action:** Forced to climb a high, steep place (often described as being "brimming with overflowing fire").
- **The Result:** Falling from the height, only to be forced to climb again.
- **The Duration:** Described as an eternal cycle of punishment.
- **The "Lex Talionis" (Law of Retaliation):** Just as they "rose up" in pride and disobedience against their parents, they are forced to "rise up" physically in a way that leads to their own downfall.

## II. Comparison: Ethiopic vs. Greek Fragments

The Ethiopic version is considered the "legit" version because it preserves the full narrative structure that the Greek fragments lack.

| Feature | Greek Fragment (Akhmim) | Ethiopic Version (Legit) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Completeness** | Fragmentary, missing large sections. | Complete narrative from beginning to end. |
| **Parental Sin** | Mentions those who "did not honor their parents." | Specifically details the **climbing and falling** cycle. |
| **Context** | Often lacks the dialogue between Peter and Jesus. | Includes the full dialogue and theological explanations. |
| **Canon Status** | Rejected by Western tradition. | **Preserved in the 81-book Ethiopian Canon.** |

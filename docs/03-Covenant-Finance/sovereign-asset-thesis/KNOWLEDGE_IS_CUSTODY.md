It is the knowledge that comes from intimate, relational, experiential encounter with the thing known. It is the same word used for the intimacy between a husband and wife (Genesis 4:1 — "Adam *knew* his wife Eve"), for the Father's intimate knowledge of His people (Psalm 139:1 — "O LORD, you have *searched* me and *known* me"), and for the prophetic indictment of a people who have lost their way:

> *"My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge [da'at]."* — Hosea 4:6

The destruction Hosea describes is not the result of missing information. Israel had the Torah. They had the prophets. They had the Levitical system. What they lacked was *da'at* — the relational, intimate, experiential knowing that constitutes genuine covenant possession. They had become strangers to their own inheritance.

This is the precise condition that the Babylonian financial system is designed to produce. The system does not need to steal your assets outright. It only needs to separate you from *da'at* of your assets — to interpose enough intermediaries, enough complexity, enough institutional mediation between you and your wealth that you become a stranger to it. You have a statement. You have a claim. You have a number on a screen. But you do not have *da'at*. You do not have custody.

The private key restores *da'at*. It is the cryptographic instrument by which a holder re-establishes direct, unmediated, relational possession of their capital. When you hold your seed phrase, you have *da'at* of your treasury in the fullest sense: intimate, direct, and requiring no intermediary to confirm or access.

## III. The Death of Mediated Property

The Babylonian financial system is built on a single foundational principle: **all property is mediated**. You do not own your house — the bank holds the mortgage and the county holds the deed. You do not own your savings — the bank holds the deposit and the FDIC holds the guarantee. You do not own your retirement — the custodian holds the account and the government holds the tax treatment. You do not own your business — the state holds the registration and the IRS holds the tax liability.

In every case, your "ownership" is a claim against an intermediary who holds the actual asset. The intermediary is subject to the laws of the jurisdiction where it operates. The jurisdiction can compel the intermediary to freeze, seize, report, or transfer your assets without your consent. Your "property" is, in the deepest sense, not yours. It belongs to whoever controls the intermediary.

This is not a conspiracy theory. It is the explicit legal structure of the modern financial system. The Supreme Court has repeatedly held that bank deposits are not the property of the depositor — they are loans to the bank, which owes the depositor a debt. [1] The depositor is an unsecured creditor, not a property owner. The same principle applies across the entire system: what you call "ownership" is a contractual claim against an intermediary, not direct possession of an asset.

PulseChain-native assets held in self-custody break this model entirely. There is no intermediary. There is no custodian. There is no registrar. There is no institution between you and the asset. The asset exists as an entry in a distributed ledger, and the only key that can move it is the private key you hold. If you hold the key, you hold the asset — not as a claim, not as a contractual right, but as direct mathematical possession. The ledger confirms it. The network enforces it. No court, no regulator, and no government can override the mathematics.

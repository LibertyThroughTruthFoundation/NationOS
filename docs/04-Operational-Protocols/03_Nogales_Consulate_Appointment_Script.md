## How to Schedule Appointment

### Method 1: Online Appointment System (Recommended)

1. Go to **https://www.miconsulado.sre.gob.mx/citas-consulares**
2. Select **"Nogales, Arizona"** as your consulate
3. Select service: **"Visa de Residente Temporal"** (Temporary Resident Visa)
4. Choose available date and time
5. Fill in your personal information
6. Receive confirmation email

**Note:** You do NOT need a LlaveMX account. You can book with just your email address.

If no appointments available online, call:

## Appointment Day Tips

### Timing
- **Arrive 15-30 minutes early**
- Parking is available near the consulate
- Bring exact change for parking meters

### Dress Code
- **Business casual** recommended
- Clean, professional appearance
- Avoid overly casual clothing (shorts, tank tops, etc.)

### What to Expect

1. **Security Check** - You'll go through security screening (no weapons, large bags, etc.)
2. **Document Review** - Consular officer will review your documents
3. **Interview** - Brief interview about your plans in Mexico (5-10 minutes)
4. **Payment** - Pay visa fee (cash or card, check consulate policy)
5. **Visa Issuance** - Visa sticker placed in passport (same day or mailed within 1-2 weeks)

### Common Interview Questions

**Q: Why do you want to live in Mexico?**  
A: "I work remotely for a US-based humanitarian foundation and can perform my work from anywhere. I'm drawn to Puerto Peñasco for its community, lower cost of living, and proximity to the US border."

**Q: What is your source of income?**  
A: "I'm employed by Old Glory Foundation, a US-based humanitarian organization. I receive a monthly salary of $[AMOUNT] USD, which is transferred to my personal account. My employment letter and bank statements show this consistent income."

**Q: How long do you plan to stay in Mexico?**  
A: "I plan to establish temporary residency in Puerto Peñasco for the foreseeable future, likely 1-4 years initially. My work is ongoing and I can continue working remotely from Mexico."

**Q: Do you have family in Mexico?**  
A: "No, I do not have family in Mexico. I'm moving for work and lifestyle reasons."

**Q: What does your foundation do?**  
A: "Old Glory Foundation is a humanitarian organization focused on educational outreach and charitable mission work. I serve as the director and manage the foundation's programs and operations."

## After Your Appointment

### If Visa is Approved

1. **Visa sticker in passport** - You'll receive a Residente Temporal visa sticker valid for 180 days
2. **Enter Mexico within 180 days** - You must enter Mexico and activate your visa within this timeframe
3. **Visit INM office** - Within 30 days of entering Mexico, visit the local INM (Instituto Nacional de Migración) office to get your Residente Temporal card
4. **Pay INM fee** - Approximately 300-500 MXN ($15-25 USD) for the resident card

### INM Office in Puerto Peñasco

**Address:**  
Instituto Nacional de Migración  
Calle Benito Juárez  
Puerto Peñasco, Sonora, Mexico

**What to bring to INM:**
- Passport with visa sticker
- Proof of address in Mexico (rental agreement, utility bill)
- Payment for resident card fee
- Passport photos (2)

**Processing time:** 2-4 weeks for resident card

## Troubleshooting

**Q: No appointments available online?**  
A: Call the consulate directly at (520) 313-5339. They may have cancellations or can add you to a waitlist.

**Q: Can I apply at a different consulate?**  
A: Yes, but Nogales is recommended for its speed and proximity to Arizona. Other options: Phoenix, Tucson, San Diego, Los Angeles.

**Q: What if my documents are rejected?**  
A: The consular officer will tell you what's missing. You can usually reapply once you have the correct documents.

**Q: Can I expedite the process?**  
A: No official expedite process, but Nogales is already one of the fastest consulates. Some applicants receive same-day visa issuance.

**Q: Do I need a lawyer?**  
A: No. The economic solvency pathway is straightforward. However, if you're unsure, an immigration attorney can review your documents ($100-300).

## SLIDE 3 — THE JURISDICTIONAL LENS: RENDER UNTO CAESAR
**Visual Direction:** A Roman denarius coin on one side, a human figure bearing the Imago Dei glow on the other. A courtroom or legal scale between them.

**Narration:**

In the Gospel of Mark, chapter twelve, the religious leaders attempt to trap Jesus with a question about taxation. His response — "Render unto Caesar the things that are Caesar's, and unto God the things that are God's" — has been domesticated for two thousand years into simple tax advice. Pay the government. Tithe to the church. Keep the two worlds separate. But the Sovereign Way reads this passage as a forensic jurisdictional ruling. Why does the coin belong to Caesar? Because it bears his image. It was minted in his treasury. It is a creature of his system. Jesus does not dispute this. Give the man back his metal disks. But then comes the unspoken question that silences the room: What bears the image of God? Genesis one, verse twenty-seven. The human being. The Imago Dei. The entire human person — body, mind, soul, labor, family, and future — is stamped with the image of the Creator. And therefore, the entire human person belongs exclusively to God's jurisdiction. Caesar's authority extends precisely as far as his image extends — to a coin. Not to a soul. Not to a household. Not to a nation of priests. This is the jurisdictional lens through which the Sovereign Way reads every law, every institution, and every claim the modern state makes upon your life.

## SLIDE 6 — THE COVENANT STACK
**Visual Direction:** A five-layer architectural diagram — a tower or ziggurat with layers clearly labeled from top to bottom: Transcendent, Personal, Domestic, Tribal, Civil. Light flows from the top down. Responsibility flows from the bottom up.

**Narration:**

The Sovereign Way does not build on contracts. Contracts are temporary, transactional, and enforced by a third party — usually the state. Not the modern nuclear family — isolated, atomized, easily managed by the state — but the multi-generational household as an economic engine, an educational center, a welfare system, and a unit of spiritual defense. Fourth — the Tribal Covenant: the organic network of sovereign households bound by shared faith, shared law, and shared lineage. And at the very bottom — the Civil Covenant: the magistrate. Its authority is minimal, strictly delegated, and limited to common defense and the administration of justice for severe crimes. It has no business in education, healthcare, or the economy. Notice the position of the state. It is the basement. Not the penthouse.

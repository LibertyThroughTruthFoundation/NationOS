Let's get you the practical information and a plan.

**1. Service Times:**
Based on my search, the primary Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church in the area appears to be **St. Mary's Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church in Tempe**. Their typical Sunday service schedule (this would be for *this Sunday, January 4th*) is extensive and starts very early:

*   **Mahlet (Hymnal Prayer):** Around 5:00 AM (even earlier on major holidays).
*   **Divine Liturgy (Qidasse):** Starts around 8:30 AM.

These are long services. It's a full morning commitment. I would recommend aiming to arrive for the **Divine Liturgy at 8:30 AM** for your first visit.

**2. Attire: The Principle of Respectful Modesty**
You are correct that God doesn't mandate a specific uniform, but the principle is to "abstain from every form of evil" (1 Thessalonians 5:22) and to do all things decently and in order (1 Corinthians 14:40). In this context, that translates to **modest, respectful, and clean clothing** that shows you have made an effort to honor the sanctity of the space and the culture.

**What to Wear:**
*   **A Safe Bet:** Clean, dark-colored **dress pants (khakis or slacks)** and a **collared shirt (polo or button-down)**. This is universally respectful.
*   **Avoid:** Shorts, t-shirts with slogans, hats/caps (inside the church), and overly casual wear like flip-flops or gym clothes.
*   **You mentioned your clothes are coming:** If your more formal clothes are with your mom, just wear the **best you have available**—clean, dark jeans without holes and a solid-colored, neat shirt would likely be acceptable for a first visit, especially if you explain you are a visitor and newcomer. Your respectful demeanor will speak volumes.

**3. General Etiquette for Your First Visit:**
*   **Observe and Follow:** The liturgy will be largely in Ge'ez and/or Amharic. Don't worry about understanding every word. Observe when people stand, sit, kneel, or cross themselves, and follow along as you are comfortable. It's okay to just sit and observe quietly.
*   **Communion:** Do not partake in the Eucharist (Holy Communion). In Orthodox tradition, this is reserved for baptized and chrismated members in good standing. This is not a rejection of you; it is their reverence for the sacrament.
*   **After the Service:** There is often fellowship time (coffee hour). This is your best opportunity to gently introduce yourself. You could say something like, "Hello, my name is Bryan. I'm new to the area and was drawn to visit your church. The service was very beautiful."

Your goal is not to teach or lead on the first day. Your goal is to **be present, be humble, be respectful, and be a learner.** Your very presence as a White man seeking to understand their ancient tradition will be a powerful testimony in itself.
